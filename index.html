Patient care

Your visit — from booking to report

A clear, calm pathway through your CT appointment. Bring your doctor’s request note and any prior scans if you have them.

  1. 1

    Book

    Call, WhatsApp, or walk in. We’ll suggest a slot and tell you if fasting or contrast prep applies.

  2. 2

    Check‑in

    Arrive a few minutes early with ID and papers. Our front desk confirms your study and answers questions.

  3. 3

    Scan

    Technologist guides you on the table. Most scans are quick; you may hear gentle whirring from the gantry.

  4. 4

    Report

    Images are reviewed by our consultants. Reports are prepared promptly for you and your referring doctor.

Fasting & diet

Some abdomen or contrast studies need a short fast. We’ll confirm when you book — follow the instructions given for your exam only.

Contrast (dye) studies

If your scan uses intravenous contrast, tell us about allergies, kidney issues, or diabetes medication. Our team screens safety before the injector is used.

Metal & clothing

Remove metal objects near the area being scanned when asked. Comfortable clothing helps you stay relaxed during the procedure.

Diabetics & special cases

Mention pregnancy or severe allergies when scheduling. We coordinate timing with your physician’s advice when needed.

General information only — not a substitute for your doctor’s instructions. For medical decisions, always follow your treating physician.

Questions patients often ask

How long does a CT scan take?
Most examinations are finished in a few minutes on the table; total time at the centre depends on preparation and whether contrast is required. We’ll give you an estimate when you book.
Will the scan hurt? Is it claustrophobic?
CT is non-invasive and usually painless. The opening is relatively open compared with MRI; if you’re anxious, tell our staff — we’ll help you feel at ease.
When will my report be ready?
We prioritise prompt, accurate reporting reviewed by consultants. Turnaround can vary by study complexity; our desk will inform you at the visit or on WhatsApp.
Do I need a doctor’s referral?
A referring doctor’s note or prescription helps us perform the correct protocol and send the report to the right clinician. Bring any document your doctor gave you.
Can I get a digital copy of my images?
Ask at the front desk about sharing images or reports with your hospital or doctor. Policies depend on format and workflow — we’ll guide you.
